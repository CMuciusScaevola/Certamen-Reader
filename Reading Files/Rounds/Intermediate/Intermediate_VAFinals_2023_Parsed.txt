Although it seemed like Caligula was about to launch an invasion of Britain, what did he command his troops to do instead?	 ANSWER: ATTACK THE SEA / COLLECT SEASHELLS
 Translate into English: Caesar imperat mīlitibus ut iter faciant. ANSWER: CAESAR ORDERS THE SOLDIERS TO MARCH (MAKE A JOURNEY). BONUS: Now translate: Mīlitēs tam fessī factī est ut iter facere nōn possent?	 ANSWER: THE SOLDIERS BECAME SO TIRED THAT THEY WERE NOT ABLE TO MARCH.

 Give the correct form of the relative pronoun needed to translate this sentence into Latin: “The teachers whom we trust most often are honest and good.” ANSWER: QUIBUS BONUS: Give the correct form of the relative pronoun needed to translate this sentence into Latin: “Those teachers whose hairs are the grayest usually work in Middle School.” ANSWER: QUŌRUM / QUĀRUM
 What is the meaning of the Emperor Vespasian’s famous quote to his son Titus pecūnia nōn olet? ANSWER: MONEY DOES NOT SMELL BONUS: Explain the meaning of Nero’s dying words: Quālis artifex pereō?	 ANSWER: I PERISH, SUCH AN ARTIST (or similar)
 Name the protagonist of a play by Sophocles who begins the play thinking that he has killed Agamemnon and Menelaus because he felt slighted by them when they did not award him Achilles’s armor, only to find out that, in his vengeful rage, he had actually slaughtered a herd of cattle and their herdsman? ANSWER: AJAX (THE GREATER) BONUS: Which deity had deluded Ajax into thinking that the cattle were men?	 ANSWER: ATHENA
 Which of the following English words do not come from the same Latin root as the others: manipulate, maneuver, manifold, manacle, manager? ANSWER: MANIFOLD BONUS: In the English word “manifold,” “mani-“ is related to the adjective “many” and “-fold” means “more.” What would be the English word derived from Latin that means “many more”?
